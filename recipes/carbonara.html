Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is a pasta dish made with eggs, hard cheese, fatty cured pork, and black pepper. It is typical of the Lazio region of Italy. The dish took its modern form and name in the middle of the 20th century. The cheese is usually pecorino romano.

Ingredients

  • 350g of spaghetti
  • 200g of guanciale
  • 4 whole medium eggs
  • 100 g of grated Pecorino Romano
  • grounded black pepper

Steps

  1. Boil the water for the pasta while you prepare the carbonara sauce. Cut the guanciale into small pieces then cook in a skillet over medium heat for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  2. Now prepare the pecorino cream. So, in a bowl put the eggs and pecorino Romano cheese. Use the whole egg, not only the egg yolk.
  3. Add some freshly ground black pepper. Then, mix quickly with a fork - or a hand whisk - until you have a creamy sauce.
  4. The water should now be boiling so add the salt and cook the spaghetti
  5. Drain the pasta when ready. Then place them in the skillet, over high heat, to season well with the guanciale.
  6. When the spaghetti and guanciale sizzle in the pan, TURN OFF THE HEAT, otherwise the eggs will overcook and you'll end up with scrambled eggs and pasta!
  7. Now quickly add the eggs and pecorino cream to the hot pasta and stir. The pan is not too hot, this way the eggs will cook without lumps.
  8. With the help of a ladle and a fork, create a pasta nest and place it on a plate.
  9. Add the guanciale (what's left in the pan), freshly ground black pepper and grated pecorino romano to taste. Serve and enjoy!
